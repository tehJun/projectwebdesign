Scales

Major Pentatonic Positions

The Pentatonic Scales are among the first scales a guitarist learns. These are the five positions for the Major Pentatonic scale. These positions can be played anywhere on the fretboard. For example if you wanted to play the A Major Pentatonic Scale, these are the five different ways you can play the scale. The black dots are the root notes of the scale. Simply play the first position with the root note being the fifth fret of the E string, which is an A note.

Minor Pentatonic Positions

This is the Minor Pentatonic Scale. Like the Major scale, there are five different shapes. The black dots represent the root note. If we wanted to play the A Minor Pentatonic Scale in the first position, we play the scale from the fifth fret of the E string which is an A note.
